Conclude the engagement

With your compilation work complete, you are ready to start preparing your conclusions and verifying that the engagement team has complied with the applicable financial reporting standards.

Complete the conclusion checklists

On the Documents page, the Forming a conclusion folder lists the final documents you'll need to complete to conclude your engagement work. Once you've completed all procedures and solicited all relevant client communications in this section, your engagement work will be complete.

  • 310 Compilation engagement - Completion checklist: Complete the procedures to confirm that the engagement team has completed all tasks associated with completing the engagement work.

  • 350 Letter from management acknowledging responsibility for the financial information: This optional letter is intended for management at your firm to sign, reconfirming the responsibility to the client outlined in your initial engagement letter.

Prepare the financial statements

At the conclusion of the engagement, you need to prepare a financial statements document for the client where you explain your findings and present a conclusion. Caseware ReviewComp has a built-in template that makes it easier for you to prepare the financial statements.

In the Issuing the financial statements section in the Documents page, there are three main documents that help you prepare the financial statements:

  • 6-10 Report transmittal - Specifies the distribution methods for the report, authorize it for release and set milestone dates such as the release and final assembly dates.
  • 6-15 Financial statements optimiser - Consists of inquiries related to terminology and presentation options.
  • 6-20 Financial statements - The financial statements auto-generated template based on the uploaded client's data and your responses in the 6-15 Financial statements optimiser.

Most of the content in the 6-20 Financial statements is already populated for you. Groups and accounts on the financial statements tables are taken from the trial balance.

However, you will need to make changes to customize the financial statements for your client. For example, similarly to the engagement letter, the financial statements also include placeholder drop down menus for you to complete.

Practice set 1 - Include analysis graphs in financial statements

A visual presentation of your client's account information is available in certain note disclosures in the financial statements to help you perform high-level analyses and identify areas of the trial balance that you need to investigate.

By default, the analysis graphs are excluded from the notes to the financial statements. To include these graphs in your notes, select Add section, then Analysis from the Excluded Content section.

You can also include the Financial Statements Analysis Graphs section in the financial statements to populate a visual representation of your client's data in a designated section.

To include the Financial Statements Analysis Graphs section in the financial statements, respond Yes to the Would like to include analysis graphs in your financial statements question in the 6-15 Financial statements optimiser.

Practice set 2 - Edit the financial statement tables

While you cannot change the specific accounts and groups that are included in a financial statement table, you can make some changes to the content that is displayed.

Many of the tables in the financial statements are populated automatically. The information in them is pulled straight from the trial balance, to make financial statement preparation easier for you. However, you may also need to enter some additional information.

Some of the lines in the financial statements are input rows. In an input row, you can enter amounts into the fields, and these amounts will be included in the final calculations for the table.

Hover the cursor over the Statement of Cash Flows and scroll down the page. Available input rows appear in grey.

Practice set 3 - Expand a line in the financial statements

Many of the financial statement tables collapse accounts into their groupings by default. You can expand lines in the financial statement to give more details about some areas of the engagement.

For example, in the balance sheet, the Long-term assets section displays totals for two areas: Property, plant and equipment and Other long-term assets. You decide that you want to give the client more information about the Property, plant and equipment group.

Select the Expand lines icon() beside Property, plant and equipment.

All of the lines under your selected group are now displayed, and you can see the amounts for each different category under Property, plant and equipment - Cost, along with the amount for Accumulated amortization of property, plant and equipment.

Note: If you want to see more details about a balance on the financial statement without changing a table, you can select the line with that balance to drill down and display more details.

Practice set 4 - Add a calculation in a table cell

You can add or edit calculations in table cells. The calculation can include numbers, data sourced from the trial balance, data sourced from the financial statements, form values (if forms are available in your product) or a combination of all.

Note: This feature is only available for cells in columns or rows with type Input.

Practice set 9 - Cross reference a note with a line in a financial statement table

You can cross reference a line in a table to one of the notes to the financial statements that holds related details or information.

For example, you want to provide a breakdown of the Property, Plant and Equipment group, but you do not want to display it on the balance sheet, so you link a line in the balance sheet to the PROPERTY AND EQUIPMENT note that you created earlier.

  1. If available, select the Statement of Financial Position from the left pane, or any alternate Statement.

  2. At the Property, plant and equipment line, select the # to display a pop-up with a list of the available notes.

  3. Scroll down to 2. PROPERTY AND EQUIPMENT and select it.

    The # is replaced with the note's number.

The Property, plant and equipment group now includes a link to the note in the Note column.
