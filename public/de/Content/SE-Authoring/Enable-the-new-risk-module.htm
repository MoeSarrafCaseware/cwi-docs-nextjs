Enable the new risk module

The current simple risk module is switching to a new user interface to support more complex engagements. The new module introduces massive functionality changes.

To enable the new risk functionality in your product, select the Information icon () at the top menu and choose Settings to open the Product Settings dialog. At the Risk | General tab, select Enable new risk functionality.

Note: The new risk module does not display if a custom form is selected for risks in the Features | Custom Forms tab. You can select None from the Risks dropdown in the tab to clear any prior selections and display the module.

Once you enable the new risk module, the new Risks page is available in the template. Also, more settings display at the Product Settings dialog:

  • Assessment

  • Fields

  • Scales

  • Signoff Option:

    • Two check boxes are available to allow you to specify how users sign off the risks report.

    • Sign off the entire report - One signoff button is available in the report for users to sign off the entire report.

    • Sign off each risk in the report - A signoff button is available in each risk card in the report. Users sign off each risk individually.

    Once you select how users sign off the risk report, you can select a custom signoff scheme. To learn more about signoff schemes, see Set up signoff schemes.

Hinweis: The following settings are only available in the Product Settings dialog if you enable the new risk functionality.

Additionally, the Risk | General tab provides an option to include the comments field in risk analysis widgets. Comments are included by default in widgets and can be disabled by selecting the checkbox.

If you have enabled the risk library for your site, you can also associate a risk library to the product in the Library Management section. For more information, see Associate a risk library to a product.

Enable the Assessment tab

To enable the Assessment tab in the Risks page, select Risk | Assessment and enable the Risk Assessment option.

Once you enable assessments, more options are available in the dialog:

  • ASSESSMENT METHOD - you can choose to enable either Area or Business Cycle. Once you enable this option, assessments cards for each available area or business cycle display in the Assessment tab.

  • Will you be testing controls? - if you enable this option, users can select whether they are testing controls for each area or cycle in the risk assessment report. If users answer that they are not testing controls, the Control risk for each assertion will be automatically set to the highest level risk. Note that this will not overwrite any manual selections made previously.

  • Inherent and Control Assessment - if you enable this option, end users can then document the inherent and control risk levels in the risk assessment report. Note that this option must be enabled to enable the Likelihood and Magnitude option. This option is enabled by default.

  • Likelihood and Magnitude - if you enable this option, end users can then document likelihood and magnitude at the Area or Cycle level in the risk assessment report.

  • Help - if you want to set up a help page for your clients to describe the functionality, fields and attributes for assessments, you can enable the Help option and add the URL. Once you enable this option, a Help icon () displays in assessment cards.

  • SIGNOFF OPTIONS - Two check boxes are available to allow you to specify how users sign off the risk assessment report.

    • Sign off the entire report - One signoff button is available in the report for users to sign off the entire report.

    • Sign off the individual assessments - A signoff button is available in each assessment card in the report. Users sign off each risk individually.

    Once you select how users sign off the risk assessment report, you can select a custom signoff scheme. To learn more about signoff schemes, see Set up signoff schemes.

  • Additional text fields - you can also enable additional custom text fields in the Assessment card, if needed. Select Custom Text Area 1 or Custom Text Area 2 to enable additional custom fields. Enter a title for the field in the TITLE text area.

  • Label Customization - you enter your custom label for the short and long form of the inherent risk, control risk and risk of material misstatements fields in the Assessment tab. You can also enter custom labels for the short and long form of the likelihood of misstatement occurring and magnitude of potential misstatement if you have enabled the Likelihood and Magnitude option.

Customize the risk fields

Under the Risks drop-down, select Fields.

To add and customize risk ID, risk description, assertions, assessment method and references fields:

  1. Select a field to include it in the risks module.

    2. For example, select to include it in the risks module.

    Once you select a field, its customization section is available in the dialog.

  2. At CUSTOM LABEL, enter your custom term for each field.

  3. For each field, select:

    • the Dialog check box to display the field in the New Risk dialog.
    • the Card check box to display the field in the risk card.
    • the Dialog and Card check boxes to display the field in the New Risk dialog and the risk card.

In the Assertions section, you can directly go to Features | Assertions to add or modify an existing assertion by selecting the Assertions link.

To add and customize risk type and risk level fields:

  1. Select Type and Level to include those fields in the risks module.

    2. Once you select a field, its customization section is available in the dialog.

  2. In the customization section for Level,

    1. enter your custom term for risk level at CUSTOM LABEL.

    2. enter your custom term for Normal, Significant and Fraud.

  3. In the customization section for Type,

    1. enter your custom term for risk type at CUSTOM LABEL.

    2. enter your custom term for Financial Statement and Assertion.

To add and customize audit response, controls and conclusion fields:

  1. Select a field to include it in the risks module.

    2. For example, select to include it in the risks module.

    Once you select a field, its customization section is available in the dialog.

  2. At CUSTOM LABEL, enter your custom term for each field.

  3. In the Conclusions customization section, enter a custom term for each conclusion type.

    1. Enter a custom term for Risk has been treated.

    2. Enter a custom term for Significant control deficiency exists.

    3. Enter a custom term for Audit response is required.

    4. Enter a custom term for Other.

If you want to set up a help page for your clients to describe the functionality, fields and attributes for risks, you can enable the Help option and add the URL. Once you enable this option, a Help icon () displays in risk cards and the New Risk dialog.

You can also choose to include the two check boxes Include in reportable items and Substantive procedures are not enough in the risk card. You can also add your own custom text for each check box.

Customize the scales and the RMM mapping

You can choose the number of scales in your product for Inherent Risks (IR), Control Risks (CR) and Risks of Material Misstatements (RMM) and customize their display names. You can also predefine the RMM mapping in the product.

Select Risk | Scales to start.

To customize scales for inherent risks:

  1. Go to the Inherent Risk section.

  2. At Assessment Scale, select the desired number of scales (between 2 and 7).

  3. At Label Customization, enter the desired custom term and short name for each scale.

    For example, you can rename Very Low to be Mild and set the short name to be ML.

To customize scales for control risks:

  1. Go to the Control Risk section.

  2. At Assessment Scale, select the desired number of scales (between 2 and 7).

  3. At Label Customization, enter the desired custom term and short name for each scale.

    For example, you can rename Very Low to be Mild and set the short name to be ML.

To customize scales for risk of material misstatements risks:

  1. Go to the RMM section.

  2. At Assessment Scale, select the desired number of scales (between 2 and 7).

  3. At Label Customization, enter the desired custom term and short name for each scale.

    For example, you can rename Very Low to be Mild and set the short name to be ML.

To predefine the RMM mapping:

  1. Go to the RMM Mapping section.

  2. Select the desired RMM scale for each combination of IR and CR scale.

    3. For example, you can preset the RMM scale to Very Low (VL) if the IR and the CR have a Very Low (VL) scale.

To customize scales for likelihood of misstatement occurring risks:

  1. Go to the Likelihood of Misstatement Occuring section.

  2. At Assessment Scale, select the desired number of scales (between 2 and 7).

  3. At Label Customization, enter the desired custom term and short name for each scale.

    For example, you can rename Very Low to Mild and set the short name to ML.

To customize scales for magnitude of potential misstatement risks:

  1. Go to the Magnitude of Potential Misstatement section.

  2. At Assessment Scale, select the desired number of scales (between 2 and 7).

  3. At Label Customization, enter the desired custom term and short name for each scale.

    For example, you can rename Very Low to Mild and set the short name to ML.
