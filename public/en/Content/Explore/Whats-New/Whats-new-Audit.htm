What’s new in Caseware Audit Spring 2025 (v 2.1883.0)

Here's a list of what's new in the Caseware Audit Spring 2025 release.

New features and enhancements

Not-for-profit engagement support

Caseware Audit now supports not-for-profit engagements. Note that the Organization Type of your entity is required to be set as Registered Charity or Not For Profit to display the not-for-profit content in your engagement. To learn how to set the Organization Type of your entity, refer to Create client entities.

The documents below have been updated to display not-for-profit content when applicable:

  • 030 Client queries – Initial client queries

  • 510 Identifying risk through understanding the entity

  • 681 NFP – Supplementary audit procedures

    • PEG FRF971 – First-time adoption of ASNPO and FRF973 Worksheet - ASNPO - Combinations by not-for-profit organizations have been authored in this document

  • C Accounts receivable, trade and other

  • U Property, plant and equipment

  • V Collections

  • W Intangible assets

  • MM Deferred contributions

  • TT Net assets

  • 705 Revenue

  • FRF906 ASPE – General financial statement presentation

  • FRF972 ASNPO – General financial statement presentation

  • 109 Financial statements optimiser

  • 110 Financial statements

Note disclosures for not-for-profit engagements

We have added additional note disclosures specific to not-for-profit engagements:

  • Purpose of the organization

  • Basis of presentation

  • Summary of significant accounting policies: Basis of presentation

  • Summary of significant accounting policies: Fund accounting

  • Summary of significant accounting policies: Revenue recognition – Restricted fund method

  • Summary of significant accounting policies: Revenue recognition – Deferral method

  • Summary of significant accounting policies: Contributed services

  • Summary of significant accounting policies: Donated goods

  • Summary of significant accounting policies: Property, plant and equipment

  • Summary of significant accounting policies: Callable debt

  • Summary of significant accounting policies: Measurement uncertainty

Financial groups for not-for-profit organizations

We have created financial groups that are common to the following not-for-profit organization types:

  • Condominiums

  • Schools

  • Churches

Additional not-for-profit financial groups will be added in future releases.

Risk library

The risk library has been updated to include additional risks specific to not-for-profit engagements.

Intangibles and goodwill

W Intangible assets and goodwill has been split into W Intangible assets and Y Goodwill. Y Goodwill is only available in for-profit engagements.

Validis integration

Performance materiality benchmark percentage

Performance materiality can now be set using a benchmark percentage of your choice instead of being restricted to the three options of High risk (65%), Moderate risk (70%), and Low risk (75%).

Customize rounding in the financial statements

Enhancements to engagement lockdown

Lockdown history as a PDF

Map dimensions during trial balance import

Central planning
