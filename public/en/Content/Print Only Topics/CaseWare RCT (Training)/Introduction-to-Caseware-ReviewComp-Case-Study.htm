Assign Accounts

After you hit import, you will be taken to the Accounts screen under Data. This is the TB imported. You should see a 21 on the top right over the Assign Accounts icon, which indicates that 21 accounts need to be assigned to financial groups. Financial groups are based on ASPE standards, part of the CW template. Accounts are assigned to the financial group they relate to and financial statements (FS) are built on the financial groups.

  1. Click on the icon with the 21. This will take you to the Assign Accounts section where all the accounts imported from the TB can be assigned to the financial groups.

  2. Click Petty cash and Domestic bank and foreign bank accounts under the Unassigned column. The accounts will move to the Selected Accounts section.

  3. As you select accounts, it will suggest what financial group the account may relate to based on the account name. Hover your mouse over Cash and click the green assign button.

  4. Another way to assign accounts is to search for an account grouping. Type accounts in the search bar. Drag and drop the Accounts receivable account in the Unassigned area onto theTrade accounts receivable in the Selected Accounts area.

    5. When assigning accounts, you will see groups and sub groups for each section.

    In order for the account date to flow through to other documents accordingly, it’s important to map the account to a subgroup if it is available.

    For example, the cash accounts are mapped to the top level.

    They should get mapped to their sub group accordingly.

  5. Assign the remaining accounts accordingly.

    6. Note: You can use the “ACME Inc-TrialBalance-FinancialGroups_Initial” PDF to ensure correct mapping. For example, there’s a current income taxes [Current Asset] and Current income tax expense. Also map to the subcategory if one exists to properly populate your statement of cash flows. For example, 6010 Depreciation of tangible assets should be mapped to the sub group Amortization of tangible assets not the main group heading.

  6. Once completed, the Net income should appear as $92,856.

    7. You have the ability to create your own financial group if you don't find a category an account relates to.

  7. Under Data | Financial Groups, click Add () and select Add group.

  8. Populate the dialogue box as shown below, then click Save.

    9. You have now created a new line item for the financial statements called Vehicle Expenses.

  9. Return to Assign accounts and search for Auto Expense.

  10. Click on 535.120 Auto Expense to unassign it and move it to the group you just created.

  11. Search for Vehicle Expenses, then drag and drop 6070 Auto Expense to this new group.

    12. You have moved the amount for Auto Expenses from Other expenses to Vehicle Expenses. On the financial statements you will see Vehicle expenses as a line item instead of Other expenses.

Section 5: Materiality and Scope

Exercise 5A: Materiality

After the TB is imported you can set materiality.

  1. Click on the Documents page to access document 2-10 Materiality.
  2. The top section of this document is where you can complete your documentation and the bottom section is where you can calculate the planning materiality for your review engagement. The Revenue and Net income basis will be there by default and the numbers will be populated based on the financial groups.
  3. Assign 5% for the benchmark for Net income and 0.5% for Revenue. You can add your own basis as you wish by selecting + Financial materiality basis or + Other basis.
  4. In the Conclusions box section under Planning, enter 12500 as the materiality level for this review.

  5. Use the navigation bar located at the top of the screen to select the Data page. Then select Accounts | Indicators |→ Materiality. The materiality icon will appear beside each account that is material.
  6. Navigate back to Documents and sign off on 2-10 Materiality.
