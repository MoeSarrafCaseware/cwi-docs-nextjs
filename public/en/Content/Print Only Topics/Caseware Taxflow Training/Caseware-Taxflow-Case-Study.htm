Prerequisites

Before you get started, ensure that you've created the XYZ Corp entity with a fictional client contact and personal email address that you can access.

Exercise Prerequisite A: Create an entity

  1. Log in to your Caseware Cloud account.

  2. On the top navigation bar, click New ().

  3. Click Entity. The New Entity dialog box appears.

  4. Populate the fields as shown under General Info.

  5. Populate the fields as shown under Phone.

  6. Populate the fields as shown under Social Profile.

  7. Populate the fields as shown under Office-Business.

  8. Click Save.

Exercise Prerequisite B: Create a contact

  1. Log in to your Caseware Cloud account.

  2. On the top navigation bar, click New ().

  3. Click Contact. The New Contact dialog appears.

  4. You will be creating yourself as a contact for this case study. Populate the fields as described under General.

    • Email (or Username) – Enter an email address that you have access to, such as your personal email. Note that this email address must be different from the email associated with your Caseware Cloud account.

    • First Name – Enter your first name.

    • Last Name – Enter your last name.

    • Entity – Select XYZ Corp and the Contact - Entity Collaborator checkbox.

  5. Click Save.

Section 1: Plan the engagement

Ensure you've completed the exercise prerequisites before you continue.

Exercise 1: Access the XYZ Corp entity

  1. Once you enter the Cloud environment, access the XYZ Corp entity and navigate to the bottom of the page to create a new Caseware Taxflow engagement.

  2. Populate the fields as described:

    • Entity – Enter XYZ Corp.

    • Name – Enter XYZ Tax Work - December 31, 2022.

    • Start Date – Enter today's date.

    • End Date – Enter the following Friday's date.

    • Budget Hours – Enter 40.

    • Budget Amount – Enter $12,000.

    • Preparer – Enter your name.

    • Reviewer – Enter your name.

    • Description – Enter an optional description.

  3. Click Save.

The engagement is created and a new tab will open.

If you need to change the Year end date to December 31, 2022, select the entity name at the top-left corner of the page. In the Engagement Properties popup, use the calendar to choose the new year end date.

To change any settings in the engagement, such as the currency, date, number, measurement unit format or signoff roles and schemes, select Information () | Settings.

To start planning your engagement, open the 1-100 Engagement set up checklist from the Documents page. Review the procedure responses to ensure that the engagement data is accurate and agrees with the information in Exercise Prerequisite A: Create an entity.

To change the entity type:

  1. Select the current entity type.

  2. On the Entity Details page, click the Edit icon ().

  3. Update the Country of Registration to United States of America.

  4. Select a supported Organization Type and click Save. You may need to refresh your engagement to view the changes.

Return to the Documents page and sign off as the Preparer and the Reviewer on 1-100 Engagement set up. To sign off, hover your cursor over 1-100 Engagement set up to display the sign off icons, then select Add sign-off.

 

The Documents page is the central hub where you'll perform most of your work. Only documents and phases that are applicable to your engagement type are visible. To see all documents available in Caseware Taxflow, select More Actions () | Hidden Items.

You can view a list of the current documents in the engagement by selecting the folder icon () on the right side of your screen. The Documents pane will open, which displays each available document grouped by phase. You can select a document to open it. You can also pin it for quick access using the pin icon (). When performing tax work within your engagement, add an issue to a document from the Add Issue icon () to indicate that additional work is required.

Section 2: Client correspondence (Queries)

When performing tax work, you may need to collaborate with a contact to retrieve additional information. The interaction between a staff member and a contact via queries can be summarized in the following figure.

To request information such as the trial balance, financial statements, taxation assessments or general entity inquiries, complete the 1-100Q Financial data and general entity information queries and responses query. You can also create and send a new query by selecting Create a document | Query.

Exercise 2: Request information

Request information from the client if needed.

  1. From the Documents page, open 1-100Q Financial data and general entity information queries and responses.

  2. The only information we need to request from the client is the trial balance. The additional items included in 1-100Q should be hidden. As you hover your mouse over each query item, the Visibility icon () will appear. To hide items, click Visibility () | Hide until Trial Balance is the only query item remaining.

    Note: You can add your own query to this document by clicking the Add Query icon (), which will appear when you hover your mouse in the space between the pre-populated queries. A pick list will appear with the types of queries that can be added.

  3. You are now ready to send the query to the client. Click Send in the upper right corner of the screen.

  4. Select a due date for the query. This is an internal date to let the client know when you would like a response. There is no consequence for replying late to an open query.

  5. Select the contact you created. Put a note in the email body for the client if you wish, then click OK.

  6. Return to your other browser or incognito tab and access your client account again.

  7. Open the email received from Caseware Cloud and click the View Details link to gain access to the query as the client, then respond accordingly.

  8. Complete the trial balance (TB) query as the client by using the Drag and drop here or click the browse button to attach the company TB, then click Submit Response.

Section 3: Import the client's data

Caseware Taxflow allows you to import trial balance and transaction data from a variety of sources.

  • Import from Working Papers using CloudBridge.

  • Import trial balance data from an engagement.

    Tip: Caseware Taxflow uses the same standard taxonomy structure as OnPoint apps. When you import the trial balance from an OnPoint Audit, OnPoint PCR or OnPoint DAS engagement, the accounts will be automatically assigned to the same financial groupings as the original source. If the groupings are not mapped, follow up with the audit & assurance team to ensure they have mapped the accounts correctly in their engagement.

  • Import trial balance data from a CSV or Excel file.

  • Import transaction data from a CSV or Excel file.

    Note: You must import the client's trial balance data before you import transactions.

  • Import from an accounting software package or structured files:

    • Import from desktop accounting software using the Cloud Import Utility.

    • Import from online accounting software (Quickbooks and Xero).

When you import data using any of the sources listed above, the financial accounts must be assigned to financial groupings.

Exercise 3A: Import the trial balance

  1. Return to the browser tab you're using for the engagement and open 1-100Q Financial data and general entity information queries and responses.

  2. Accept the TB sent by the client.

  3. On the top navigation bar, click Data.

  4. On the Data page, select the Excel or CSV import type.

  5. Choose Select file from engagement.

  6. Under Financial Reporting Document(s), select the trial balance you received from the query in the previous exercise.

  7. In the import wizard, assign the columns as shown.

  8. Click Import.

Exercise 3B: Assign accounts

After you click import in the previous exercise, you'll be taken to the Accounts screen on the Data page. This is the imported TB data. You should see a red 38 at the top-right corner of the page, which indicates that 38 financial accounts need to be assigned to financial groupings.

Note: The standardized taxonomy in Taxflow and the OnPoint solutions is XBRL-based and not numerical structure. For example the grouping for Cash is "Cash" and Accounts Receivable is "Trade Receivables".

  1. Click the icon with the red 38. The Assign Accounts screen opens, where all the accounts imported from the TB can be assigned to the financial groups.

  2. Click Petty cash under the Unassigned column. The account will move to the Selected Accounts section.

  3. As you select accounts, it will suggest what financial group the account may relate to based on the account name. Hover your mouse over Cash equivalents and click the green assign button.

    • Another way to assign accounts is to search for an account grouping. Type inventory in the search bar. Drag and drop the Inventory in the Unassigned area onto the Other inventory group in the Selected Accounts area.

    • When assigning accounts, you will see groups and sub groups for each section. In order for the account data to flow through to other documents accordingly, it's important to map the account to a subgroup if it's available.

  4. Assign the remaining accounts accordingly.

    Note: Refer to XYZ Corp TrialBalance FinancialGroups.pdf to ensure the mapping is correct.

  5. Once completed, the Net income should appear as $92,856.

  6. Return to the Documents page and sign off as the Preparer and the Reviewer on 1-500 Trial balance import.

Note: You have the ability to create your own financial group if you don't find a category that relates to the account. Under Data | Accounts or Financial Groups, click Add () and select Add account or Add group.

Section 4: Modify tax group assignments

After importing the client's data and completing the assignment to financial groups, you can review or modify the tax groups using the 1-550 Tax groups assignment form. The form includes a list of financial group accounts, account details, final balances for the current and prior years and the default tax group.

Note: As there is a standardized financial grouping structure, the solution has auto-mapped the financial line items to tax code groups.

Salaries - other is actually officer compensation. We will modify the tax group assignment of this account on the 1-550 Tax groups assignment form.

Exercise 4: Modify tax group assignments

  1. From the Documents page, open 1-550 Tax groups assignment.

  2. In the 1-550 Tax groups assignment form, select the Expand icon () next to Operating Expenses and Salaries to open the financial group account, Salaries - other, then select the Tax group field.

  3. In the override prompt, select Yes to change the tax group.

  4. Delete the current tax group name and start typing compensation. From the tax group drop-down, select the new tax group, Compensation of officers.

     

  5. The tax group field now has a red background to indicate that the default value has been overridden. When you hover over the overridden field, a tooltip appears where you have the option to remove your changes.

     

  6. Return to the Documents page and sign off as the Preparer and the Reviewer on 1-550 Tax group assignment.

    Note: If you need to create, modify or delete a tax group, select C-Corporation tax to quickly access the 1-560 C-Corporation - Tax groups form. If an account is not assigned to a tax group, a warning appears at the top of form 1-550.

    You can also access the available tax schedules from Form 1-560 or Form 1-550.

Section 5: Tax adjustments

When posting tax adjustments to your Caseware Taxflow engagement file, you can add tax reclassifying adjustments and book-to-tax reconciliation adjustments. After posting your adjustments, view them from the Tax adjustments summary page.

Exercise 5: Making tax adjustments

  1. On the navigation bar, click Tax adjustments, then + Tax adjustment. The Tax adjustment dialog will appear. Populate the tax adjustment entry as shown. The entry will save automatically.

    To add or delete a tax row, select the Add ( + ) or Delete ( - ) icon. A warning appears if your tax adjustments are out of balance.

  2. Click on the Tax schedules drop-down and select 1120C Tax schedule.

  3. The entry is reflected in the Book-to-tax reconciliation column when viewing Income and deductions.

  4. Click Schedule M-1 - Reconciliation of income (loss) per books with income per return. The entry is reflected on Schedule M-1 and the final tax income has been computed.

  5. On the top navigation bar, click Tax adjustments to view or print a summary of your tax adjustments. Click +Tax adjustment to add adjustments or modify existing tax adjustments.

  6. Return to the Documents page and sign off as the Preparer and the Reviewer on 2-200 Tax adjustments summary.

Section 6: Tax export

Caseware Taxflow allows you to create an export file based on a default account mapping or grouping to import into your tax software.

Exercise 6: Tax export

  1. From the Documents page, open 2-500 Tax export. In the 2-500 Tax export form, the export options are automatically populated from entity information and 1-100 engagement set up. Any corrections would need to be made in those areas to update the 2-500 Tax export form.

    The tax schedules that are exported depend on the entity type.

  2. Click Export. The tax export file is downloaded to your device.

  3. Import the tax export file into your third-party tax filing solution, then verify that the account balances have been correctly mapped on the tax forms and adjust any mappings within the tax software as needed.

  4. Return to the Documents page and sign off as the Preparer and the Reviewer on 2-500 Tax export.

Section 7: Complete the engagement

To complete your tax engagement, send a request to your client to review and sign the tax authorization form and the final tax return generated within your tax software. The queries are available from the Documents page, in Client correspondence 3-100Q Tax authorization form query and 3-200Q Final tax return query. Simply attach the documents from your tax software and follow the same query steps in Exercise 2. After your client returns the signed documents and you accept them, you're ready to file the return and lock down the engagement to prevent any changes.

Exercise 7: Lock down the engagement

When you are done with the engagement, you should lock down the file to prevent further changes.

  1. Click on the entity name at the top-left corner of the page, then click Lockdown.

  2. Click the Lockdown button.

  3. Click Yes to confirm.

After locking down the engagement, Locked will appear under the entity name in the top-left corner.

You can click on the entity name and select Unlock to unlock the file and make changes if needed; however, you will be required to provide a reason.
