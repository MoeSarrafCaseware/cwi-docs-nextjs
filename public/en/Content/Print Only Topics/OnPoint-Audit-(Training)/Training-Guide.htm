To use OnPoint Audit, you must have a Caseware Cloud account with access to the OnPoint Audit app.

What is OnPoint Audit?

OnPoint Audit is a purely cloud-based solution built for performing audits in the US market and is used by small and mid-sized US firms. It features a guided workflow that reduces redundancies and increases both productivity and the quality of audit engagements.

The auditor's responses to various checklists intelligently populate the client file with the relevant documents and content. Engagement team members and other stakeholders can modify the checklists and add additional steps if needed. For each engagement type, the predefined checklist procedures meet the requirements of the relevant regulatory standards.

As you work through your engagement, you can assign tasks and document requests to client contacts and staff members, all without leaving the OnPoint Audit interface. The content and workflow in OnPoint Audit provide non-authoritative guidance on applying auditing standards issued by the American Institute of CPAs (AICPA).

Add an entity

In Caseware Cloud, an entity represents the client organization or an internal department. Most entities belong to one of two types, depending on their function: client or internal. Client entities represent a client's legal entities or components. Internal entities represent departments, activities, or specializations within your business.

To create a new entity, click on the plus sign in the upper right-hand corner.

A Create Entity dialog will appear, prompting you to enter the necessary information to create a new entity (client). Only certain users have access to this dialog.

Depending on how security permissions are configured within the Caseware Cloud site, after creating the entity, we may need to share it with the relevant staff members so they can create engagements for the entity. To do this, click the share icon, select the staff member, and assign them the appropriate role. This will grant the staff member full access to the entity. Then, click Share to complete the process.

If you do not have permission to create new entities in your Caseware Cloud site, you will need to ask your administrator to create the entity for you and ensure that you are granted access to it.

Add staff

Staff include managers, partners, and junior or senior staff accountants or auditors who use Caseware Cloud Apps to perform audits, reviews, compilations, or other types of engagements related to assurance, financial reporting, tax, and compliance. Staff members typically take on the roles of preparer, reviewer, and second reviewer in Cloud engagements.

Add contacts

Contacts are points of contact on the client side who are responsible for collaborating with engagement team members. In Caseware Cloud Apps, contacts typically:

  • Respond to the engagement team's questions

  • Provide requested documents and information to the engagement team

  • Perform other collaboration tasks, such as commenting on activities or questions from staff

The information needed to create a new contact includes:

  • Email address

  • First name

  • Last name

  • Entity - select the entity the user will be associated with

  • Entity role - select Entity Collaborator so they can respond to requests or questions from staff

Create an engagement

An engagement is a specific project or task that an accounting firm undertakes for a client. This could be an audit, a review, a tax filing, or any other service the firm provides. In Caseware Cloud, an engagement can be thought of as a workspace for that project. Within an entity, all related engagements are stored, including prior-year completed engagements.

When a new engagement is created, the start and end dates are defined, and any relevant budget information is entered. The engagement is where all the work for that project takes place. It's where you can manage documents, complete checklists, set deadlines and budgets, and more.

Checklists

OnPoint Audit is made up of a series of checklists that, by default, are only visible based on the information submitted in other templates. In other words, as you respond to questions in a checklist, those responses may trigger the visibility of other checklists related to that area of the audit. For example, most checklists remain hidden until the audit has been accepted in the 1-100 Engagement - Acceptance/Continuance checklist.

To view hidden checklists (those that have not yet become visible based on your selections), you can select the three dots in the Documents section and then choose Hidden items. This will display all available documents.

Documents that are hidden by default have a crossed-out eye icon next to them.

It can be helpful to understand why a document is hidden. By selecting the icon, you can view the visibility settings, which explain why the document is or is not visible.

In this example, the visibility of the document is determined by the 1-100 Engagement - Acceptance/Continuance template. At the bottom, it lists the section of the template it relates to, along with the expected response and the current response. Since the expected and current responses differ, the document is not visible by default. Once the response changes to match the expected value, the checklist will become visible. In some cases, a checklist must meet multiple criteria to become visible.

To hide the checklists again, select the three dots in the Documents section and disable Hidden items. It is generally best to keep them hidden so the focus remains on the documents that are currently needed, rather than displaying all documents.

Checklists will generally have two or more tabs, including a Procedures tab, which contains the questions that need to be answered or documented, and a Guidance tab, which provides additional information about the checklist along with references to supporting documents. Depending on the type of checklist, other tabs might also be present.

Generally, there are different types of questions within a checklist:

  • Manual - where the auditor documents responses directly

  • Picklist - where the auditor selects one item from a list (e.g., Yes, No, N/A)

  • Multi-Picklist - where the auditor selects multiple items from a list

Within a checklist, the left side contains a Document Map, which allows you to jump to different sections by selecting the corresponding link. The Document Map can be hidden by selecting the arrow icon at the bottom of the map.

Next to many of the questions, there is a light bulb icon. This indicates that additional information is available for that question. Clicking the light bulb opens guidance on the left-hand side, which may include further explanations or links to source material.

While we've seen how document checklists can be hidden in the Documents tab, individual questions can also be hidden within a checklist. To view hidden questions, select the three dots in the upper right-hand corner and choose Hidden items.

Hidden questions will appear with a specific icon.

To understand why a question is hidden, select the eyeball icon to view its visibility settings. These work the same way as the visibility settings for document checklists.

In the upper right-hand corner, there are several icons:

  • The flag icon is for issues. Issues can be created at the question, document, or overall entity level.

  • The printer icon allows you to print the document checklist.

  • The tools icon provides access to review tools.

  • The pencil icon allows you to add additional procedures to the checklist if needed.

  • The final icon allows you to collapse or expand the different sections within the document.

Engagement acceptance and setup

1-100 Engagement Acceptance/Continuance

In order to start the audit, the 1-100 Engagement - Acceptance/Continuance document must be completed. The objective of this document is "To assess whether the level of engagement risk is acceptable to the firm; the firm has the necessary competencies; the firm and staff comply with the independence requirements; and the engagement preconditions have been met."

Once you understand how the document works, it is time to begin filling in the first document, namely 1-100 Engagement - Acceptance/Continuance. This document is broken down into several sections, with others added as questions are answered:

  • Description of engagement

  • Engagement risk assessment

  • Engagement risk factors

  • Engagement preconditions

  • Management integrity

The following is a sample of responses for the first section (note that these may not reflect your specific audit and should be adjusted accordingly).

The Entity type is pre-populated based on the information entered when the entity was created. The Financial reporting framework refers to the specific framework used by the entity; you must select the most appropriate one.

Continuance/New audit is important, as it drives many of the checklists. The question asks, "Is this a continuance of an audit engagement?" If the answer is no, the checklists for a new engagement will be used. If the answer is yes, the checklists for a continuing audit are used.

Many of the responses also determine how the financial statements will be displayed. For example, if you select yes under Consolidation to indicate that this audit engagement is for a consolidated or combined entity, then the financial statements will reflect that they are consolidated.

The next section relates to the engagement risk assessment and asks, "Would accepting/continuing with this engagement violate any of the firm's quality assurance policies on client acceptance/continuance or any ethical requirements (including independence)?" The following section asks whether any risks exist that would impact engagement acceptance or continuance.

As the questions are answered, the auditor's initials will appear next to each question to indicate who responded.

Hovering your cursor over the initials will display additional information, such as the full name of the user and the date of the sign-off.

At the bottom of the form, there are Conclusion and Partner Acknowledgement sections. The conclusion addresses the overall risk assessment and whether an engagement quality control review is required.

The Partner Acknowledgement either accepts the engagement, at which point all necessary checklists to begin the audit become accessible, or indicates that the engagement is not accepted.

Assign accounts

This is where any accounts will be assigned. You are matching the accounts imported from the trial balance to the OnPoint Audit accounts. In this example, the cash amount of $146 was not matched to a proper account.

There are several ways to assign an account. The first method is to drag the unassigned account over the financial groups. For example, if you drag the cash account over Current Assets, it will expand the group. You can then drag it over Cash and Cash Equivalents, and finally over Cash, where you release the unassigned account to assign it.

Another method is to highlight the unassigned account and then select the appropriate account in the financial groups. In this case, if Cash is selected, an option will appear to assign the account.

Click Assign, and the cash account will be assigned accordingly.

If a mistake is made and an account is not assigned properly, you can select the account and choose the option to unassign it. You can then reassign it to the correct account.

Financial groups

It is possible to create additional financial groups, as the financial groups needed for your client might not currently exist. To create a financial group, select the plus sign in the upper right corner. Then select the Add group option.

There are two fields that need to be completed: the number and the name. The number is the group reference number and must be unique. In this example, we will add a Canadian bank account to the Cash equivalents section. The number cannot contain any spaces and may only include letters, periods, or dashes.

Next, assign it a parent group. This allows the new group to roll up within the financial statements.

There are also other grouping options available, such as non-departmental income statement, group type, breakdown by dimension, and area.

Once all the information has been entered, select Save. If you return to Assign Accounts, the Canadian bank account is now available and appears under Cash equivalents.

Issues (review notes)

In engagements, you can add and complete review notes. Review notes help signal to the engagement team that additional work is required on a document or that specific tasks must be completed for the engagement.

Review notes can be added to an engagement workspace (entity) or to documents, checklists, and other engagement artifacts within the engagement file.

Issues are categorized as follows:

  • To do

  • Error

  • Letter

  • Deficiency

  • Review

  • Note

  • Consideration

  • Consideration for next year

  • Contact comment

Issues can be created at the engagement level, document level, or for specific questions within a document. The creation process is the same in all three scenarios. Select the Issues icon to open the issue form, where you can enter a description and choose the issue type from the dropdown menu.

You can flag a person within the issue by using the @ symbol, which opens a user list so you can tag the appropriate person(s). For engagement-level issues, the Issue button is located in the upper left corner next to the name of the entity.

To view all issues in one place, select the Issues tab.

Materiality

The Materiality template allows you to determine overall materiality and the performance materiality required to reduce, to an acceptably low level, the probability that the aggregate of uncorrected or undetected misstatements exceeds overall materiality.

This document is broken down into the following sections:

  • Principal users of the financial statements

  • Overall materiality

  • Qualitative disclosures

  • Performance materiality

  • Trivial misstatement

  • Materiality for specific circumstances

  • Performance materiality for specific circumstances

Principal users of the financial statements

This section allows for the listing of the principal users of the financial statements and any factors to consider for these users.

Overall materiality

This section allows for calculating possible materialities based on different scenarios. By default, the Revenue, Assets, and Accounts fields will be populated with the current and prior year amounts. A benchmark percentage can then be applied. Clicking the lightbulb icon next to the Overall materiality title will display additional documentation, including suggested benchmark percentages.

If the maximum percentage is used for each benchmark, the following would result:

In this instance, an auditor might select $200,000 as the planning materiality based on the assets. This amount would be entered into the planning materiality field and would then appear in the upper right-hand corner under Net Income.

There are also other options for estimating materiality. It can be based on another financial materiality basis, a custom formula, or another relevant basis.

Qualitative disclosures

This section allows you to add factors that could result in a material misstatement in qualitative disclosures.

Performance materiality

Performance materiality is the amount or amounts set by the auditor at less than materiality for the financial statements as a whole, to reduce to an appropriately low level the probability that the aggregate of uncorrected and undetected misstatements exceeds materiality for the financial statements as a whole.

If applicable, performance materiality also refers to the amount or amounts set by the auditor at less than the materiality level or levels for particular classes of transactions, account balances, or disclosures.

Set performance materiality at an amount that is based on, but lower than, overall materiality (for example, between 50-75% of overall materiality). Use professional judgment based on expectations of misstatements that could arise in the current period. Consider business and fraud risks identified, results of performing risk assessment procedures, and the nature and extent of misstatements identified in previous audits.

Entering the benchmark percentage applied will update the planning materiality as well as update the materiality shown in the upper right corner.

Trivial misstatement

Trivial misstatements represent misstatements which, in the auditor's judgment, are not material to the financial statements, either individually or when aggregated with other such misstatements, even after considering the possibility of further undetected misstatements.

The auditor should accumulate misstatements identified during the audit, other than those that are clearly trivial.

Materiality for specific circumstances

This section is for financial statements that require a lower level of materiality. It addresses specific user expectations that call for a materiality level lower than overall materiality for certain areas.

This materiality can be based on financial materiality, specific financial areas, or another relevant basis.

Performance materiality for specific circumstances

The Performance materiality for specific circumstances section calculates performance materiality based on the Materiality for specific circumstances section.

Financial statement areas worksheet

The Financial statement areas worksheet includes one document. This document links to the Risk & controls tab and displays the financial statement-level risks. It breaks down the financial statements by category, showing the RMM (risk of material misstatement) for each item, the current year (CY) and prior year (PY) balances, along with the variance. Selecting the name of any item will open a form that displays the assertions, allowing the auditor to assess them based on completeness, existence, accuracy, valuation, and presentation and disclosure.

If any assertion-level risks have previously been identified, they will be shown in this section.

This document can also be filtered by type, such as whether the risks are normal, significant, or fraud-related. It can also be filtered based on materiality.

The columns can be customized as needed.

Analytics Hub

The Analytics Hub can be accessed through the Analytics Hub tab. This provides access to all analytical tests.

The analytical tests can also be accessed through document 6-200. There is an additional icon next to many of the items in the document. By clicking on this icon, you can access the analytical tests related to that step.

For example, if you select the icon for significant changes in monetary amounts, you will be taken to the Analytics Hub, where the relevant tests will be displayed.

Selecting a test will open the documentation and configuration section, where you can configure the test. For instance, if the JE-High value amount test is selected, you will see an explanation of the test and a section for configuration.

For this test, you must select a numeric field (Amount, in this case) and enter a high-value threshold. In this example, the test will extract all transactions greater than 10,000. Different tests will have different configuration options.

You can also filter the dataset so that the test is only run on a portion of the data rather than the entire dataset.

The analytical details section provides the purpose of the test.

Finally, there is the linkage section.

Once one or more tests have been selected, click Run to start the analysis. When the analysis is complete, the Status section will update with a green checkmark and a success message next to the test.

The Results tab will also become available.

Instead of running analytics by group, you can select the Analytics Hub to run all analytics as a batch using the default configuration. You can also update the configuration for any of the tests as needed.

Lockdown

Once the audit is complete, the engagement needs to be locked down to prevent any further changes.

Lockdown can be accessed by selecting the company's name in the upper left-hand corner.

Select Lockdown, and you will be asked to confirm whether you want to lock the engagement. Selecting Yes will lock the engagement.

If you have the appropriate admin rights, you can unlock the engagement for a limited time to make changes.

When performing the unlock, a reason must be provided. The unlock will only last for the current session.

The lock function can also be configured to trigger automatically a set number of days after the end of the engagement.
