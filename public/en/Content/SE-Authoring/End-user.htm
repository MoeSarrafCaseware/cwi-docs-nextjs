End users

End users are managers, partners, junior or senior staff accountants or auditors, etc. who use the template to perform engagements for their firm's clients. In SE products, those people take on the preparer, reviewer and second reviewer roles.

To learn more about setting up end users in your SE product, see Onboarding staff.

Goal: Perform audit, review and compilation or other types of engagements related to assurance, financial reporting, tax and compliance.

Manager Partner

Manager Partners are responsible for establishing the firm's policies and procedures to promote an internal culture that recognizes quality is essential in performing engagements.

Role highlights:

  • Approve the acceptance of a professional services engagement.
  • Ensure the firm's compliance with professional standards and applicable legal and regulatory requirements.
  • Decide which technology to use in the practice.

Engagement Partner

Engagement Partners are responsible for the engagement's performance. In addition to approving the acceptance of a profession services agreement, they have authority from a professional, legal or regulatory body to sign the issued report (on behalf of the firm).

Role highlights:

  • Review the budget to actual time on the engagement.
  • Ensure engagement is done in accordance with Assurance Standards and other regulatory requirements.
  • Reviews client acceptance or continuance (reacceptance) policies.
  • Approve the engagement plan and conclusions reached.
  • Review the key documents in the engagement.
  • Review and approves deliverables such as the Financial Statements, Letter to Management and audit findings (controls and business processes improvements).
  • Review risks, controls, estimates and related conclusions.
  • Create, manage and close issues.
  • Mentor managers and staff.

Engagement quality control review (EQCR)

Engagement Quality Control Review (EQCR) is conducted by internal or external qualified individuals that are not part of the engagement team and have sufficient and appropriate experience to objectively evaluate the significant judgments the engagement team has made and the conclusions reached.

ECQR must be completed prior to the dating of the engagement report. It can be conducted by a partner in the firm or an external qualified individual or a team of external individuals.

Role highlights:

  • Discuss significant matters with the engagement partner.
  • Review the financial statements or other information, the engagement report and the selected engagement documentation.
  • Evaluate the conclusions reached in preparing the report and considerations of whether the report is appropriate.
  • Create, manage and close issues.
  • Consultant
  • Consultants are individuals with specialized expertise that can assist partners and staff with the technical interpretations of certain areas, such as Tax, Valuations, Actuarial and IT.
  • Role highlights:
  • Review significant technical, difficult or contentious document matters.
  • Create, manage and close issues.

Senior Manager

Senior Managers assist staff with technical interpretations of accounting and assurance standards, as well as liaise with partners.

Role highlights:

  • Perform either a detail or general review of engagement documents (depending on complexity and size of the engagement team).
  • Escalate any engagement issues.
  • Project-manage multiple engagements.
  • Review the budget to actual time on the engagement.
  • Create, manage and close issues.
  • Coach and mentor staff.

Audit Manager

Audit Managers assist staff with technical interpretations of accounting and assurance standards. They also review the engagements to ensure teams follow the firm policies and procedures and make appropriate audit and assurance findings.

Role highlights:

  • Perform either a detail or general review of engagement documents (depending on complexity and size of the engagement team).
  • Escalate any engagement issues
  • Project-manage multiple engagements.
  • Review the budget to actual time on the engagement.
  • Create, manage and close issues.
  • Coach and mentor staff.

Senior Accountant

Senior Accountants plan the engagement, set up the file, draft the budget and propose staffing. They have a detailed knowledge of the applicable accounting and assurance standards, as well as monitor the progress of the deliverables. They are also the key point of contact between the partners, managers and clients regarding issues, deliverables, adjustments, etc.

Role highlights:

  • Assign tasks to the engagement team members.
  • Communicate with clients, internal staff and partners.
  • Address risk or procedures.
  • Complete the execution and conclusion areas of the engagement.
  • Supervise and review work of junior staff.
  • Escalates any engagement issues.
  • Project-manage the engagement.
  • Review budget to actual time on the engagement.

Accountant

Staff accountants perform work on the less complex execution areas of the engagement, for example the Cash and Expenses Testing in a audit engagement.

Role highlights:

  • Complete the execution areas of the engagement as assigned by the senior accountant.
  • Address issues related to work assigned.

Client Contact

Client contacts are point of contact at the client-side that are responsible for collaborating with the engagement team members.

Role highlights:

  • Respond to the engagement team questions.
  • Provide requested documents to the engagement team.
